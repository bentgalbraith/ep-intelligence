EPI Program & Access Agreement

This EPI Program & Access Agreement (this “Agreement”) is entered into by and between Estate Planning Intelligence LLC, a Texas limited liability company (“EPI”), and the law firm or legal organization accepting this Agreement through EPI’s onboarding flow (the “Firm”). EPI and the Firm may each be referred to as a “Party” and collectively as the “Parties.”

This Agreement is effective as of the date the Firm accepts it through EPI’s onboarding flow (the “Effective Date”).

1. Program Access

EPI provides access to software tools, AI-enabled workflows, document automation tools, document analysis tools, drafting-support tools, workflow support, and related product-development and advisory access for estate planning practices (collectively, the “Program”).

Subject to the Firm’s compliance with this Agreement and timely payment of applicable fees, EPI grants the Firm a limited, non-exclusive, non-transferable, revocable right to access and use the Program during the term of this Agreement solely for the Firm’s internal operations.

The Program includes access to EPI’s then-current tools generally made available to participating firms. EPI will also invite the Firm to provide feedback, test in-development tools, participate in product discussions, and provide input regarding future EPI tools and workflows.

2. Nature of Tools

The Firm acknowledges that the Program includes a mix of tools and workflows at different stages of development. Some tools may be more mature and production-ready, while others may be experimental, developing, newly released, or subject to ongoing improvement.

EPI may modify, update, replace, suspend, limit, or discontinue tools, features, workflows, integrations, interfaces, prompts, models, processes, or functionality at any time in EPI’s discretion, subject to the terms of this Agreement. EPI does not guarantee that any particular tool, feature, workflow, or functionality will remain available indefinitely.

3. Authorized Users

The Firm may permit its attorneys, employees, contractors, and other personnel working for or on behalf of the Firm to access and use the Program, provided that each such user is using the Program solely for the Firm’s internal business purposes and is subject to the Firm’s supervision and control.

The Firm is responsible for all access to and use of the Program by its authorized users. The Firm may not share login credentials, access links, accounts, or Program access with any person or entity outside the Firm.

The Firm may not resell, sublicense, distribute, rent, lease, or otherwise make the Program available to third parties.

4. Fees, Billing, and Payment

The Firm shall pay EPI a Program fee of $1,500 per month, payable monthly in advance.

Payment is due immediately upon signup and will be processed through Stripe or another payment processor designated by EPI. The Firm may pay by card or ACH, subject to the payment options made available during onboarding.

The Firm authorizes EPI and its payment processor to charge the Firm’s selected payment method on a recurring monthly basis unless and until the Firm’s subscription is cancelled in accordance with this Agreement.

Unless otherwise stated by EPI in writing, each monthly subscription period renews on the same calendar day of each month as the Firm’s original signup date. If a month does not contain that calendar day, renewal may occur on the last day of that month.

If payment fails, is declined, is reversed, is disputed through a chargeback, or is not timely received, EPI may suspend or terminate the Firm’s access to the Program. EPI may also require the Firm to update its payment method before access is restored.

5. Cancellation and Termination

This Agreement is month-to-month.

Either Party may cancel this Agreement at any time before the next monthly renewal. If the Firm cancels before the next monthly renewal, the Firm will retain access through the end of the then-current paid subscription month unless EPI determines that earlier termination is necessary due to breach, nonpayment, chargeback, credential sharing, security risk, unlawful use, unauthorized use, violation of confidentiality obligations, infringement of EPI’s rights, or use of the Program to build, assist, or improve a competing product or service.

Fees paid by the Firm are not refundable if the Firm cancels, except as expressly provided in this Agreement or otherwise agreed by EPI in writing.

If EPI terminates the Firm’s participation for any reason other than the Firm’s breach, misuse, nonpayment, chargeback, security risk, or violation of this Agreement, EPI will refund the full monthly fee paid by the Firm for the then-current subscription month. EPI will process such refund to the original payment method within 10 business days after termination.

EPI may suspend or terminate the Firm’s access immediately if EPI reasonably believes that the Firm or any authorized user has:

  • breached this Agreement;
  • failed to pay amounts owed;
  • initiated an improper chargeback or payment dispute;
  • shared credentials, accounts, access links, or Program access with unauthorized persons;
  • created or contributed to a security risk;
  • used the Program for unlawful, unauthorized, deceptive, abusive, harmful, or infringing purposes;
  • violated confidentiality obligations;
  • infringed or misused EPI’s intellectual property or proprietary rights;
  • used or attempted to use the Program to build, assist, or improve a competing product or service;
  • interfered with, disrupted, overloaded, or attempted to gain unauthorized access to the Program or EPI’s systems; or
  • engaged in conduct that materially interferes with EPI’s operations, systems, security, or ability to provide the Program.

Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, the Firm’s right to access and use the Program will end.

Support and advisory access are intended to help the Firm use and provide feedback on the Program. They do not constitute legal advice, malpractice advice, ethics advice, or professional responsibility advice.
